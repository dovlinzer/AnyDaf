# Menachot 89 — Daf Yomi Shiur

## Menorah Structure

### Lamps Not Attached

We begin at the very bottom of 88b, concluding a discussion about the מנורה, since one of the measures under consideration was the חצי לוג of oil for each lamp. Rabbi Yehoshua ben Karchah states: מנורה באה מן הכיכר ואין מלקחיה ומחתותיה ונרותיה באין מן הכיכר — the מנורה itself came from the כיכר, the talent of gold, but the tongs, the small shovels used for scraping and cleaning, and the lamps themselves did not come from the כיכר. The practical implication is significant: it is considerably easier to clean the lamps if they are not physically attached to the מנורה.

The reasoning flows from the way the תורה assigns the כיכר. The תורה says two things about the מנורה: that it requires a כיכר of gold, and that it is מקשה — beaten, formed as one unit. The assumption is that these two characteristics belong together. Rabbi Yehoshua ben Karchah therefore holds that only the מנורה proper was formed from the כיכר and was part of that unified structure, while the implements and the lamps were not.

### Unified Menorah, Separat…

What, then, does the phrase אלא מה אני מקיים את כל הכלים האלה — "what do I make of all these vessels" — mean, given that the תורה speaks of both the כיכר and all these vessels? The answer is שהיו כלי זהב — it means they are of gold, not that they are from the כיכר and not that they are attached. The גמרא objects: but that they are gold is already explicit, as the verse states ועשית את נרותיה שבעה... מלקחיה ומחתותיה זהב טהור. If the goldness of the implements is already stated explicitly, what purpose remains for the verse?

### Pure Gold Lips

The גמרא answers: לא נצרכה אלא לפי נרות — the verse is needed only for the lips of the lamps themselves. One might have thought — סלקא דעתך אמינא — that since the lips of the lamps become black from direct contact with fire, and since the תורה is concerned with the money of Israel, התורה חסה על ממונם של ישראל, they need not be made of pure gold. Rashi explains this to mean a gold alloy rather than pure gold, since pure gold would be damaged precisely where the fire burns. Therefore, קא משמע לן: the verse teaches that despite this concern, the lips too must be of pure gold.

This also concludes our מנורה discussion, though what remains especially interesting is the question raised in the previous session: whether the lamps were physically connected, and how the מנורה was relit if a lamp went out during the night. The simple reading of the גמרא suggested one position, while the Rambam's strikingly different reading — that both the cleaning and the relighting were performed twice daily, at the onset of night and again first thing in the morning — stands against the other ראשונים and many parallel גמרות.

## Todah Oil & Ratios

### Todah Half Log

At the top of 89a, the משנה states: חצי לוג שמן לתודה — a half לוג of oil for the תודה. The normal amount of oil accompanying a מנחה is a לוג per עשרון. The תודה, however, includes עשרה עשרונות for the ten loaves of חמץ and עשרה עשרונות for the thirty loaves of מצה. The חצי לוג under discussion here applies only to the מצה loaves, meaning a half לוג for thirty loaves — עשרה עשרונות. This yields a ratio of roughly one to twenty, far less oil than the standard one-to-one ratio of a לוג per עשרון.

### Double Oil Mention

The ברייתא records Rabbi Akiva's derivation. The verse concerning the תודה reads: חלות מצות בלולות בשמן ורקיקי מצות משוחים בשמן וסולת מרבכת חלות בלולות בשמן — the word בשמן appears three times in connection with the three types of מצה loaves. Rabbi Akiva asks: why does the verse say בשמן twice? Had it said בשמן only once, I would have assumed it is like all other מנחות and receives a לוג — even though that is already a לוג for עשרה עשרונות, which is itself unusual. But since it says בשמן twice, this constitutes ריבוי אחר ריבוי, and ואין ריבוי אחר ריבוי אלא למעט — a doubled expansive formulation comes only to diminish. Hence, מיעטו הכתוב לחצי לוג — the verse reduces the amount to a half לוג.

The גמרא, however, raises a technical difficulty: is this truly ריבוי אחר ריבוי? The first בשמן is needed simply to indicate that oil is required at all — had it only said it was a מנחה, one would already know that oil is needed. So the first בשמן is already a ריבוי in its own right, and the second בשמן constitutes ריבוי אחר ריבוי, from which we derive the reduction.

### Three Oil Mentions

From here, the גמרא considers the third mention of בשמן. One might think the half לוג is divided equally among the three types of מצה: the חלות, the רקיקין, and the רביכה. This would mean taking chatzi log and dividing it into three. But כשהוא אומר בשמן ברביכה — the fact that the verse says בשמן a third time by the רביכה specifically, where שאין תלמוד לומר — there was no need to state it, tells us שריבה שמן לרביכה — the רביכה receives more oil than the חלות and the רקיקין.

The practical calculation: מביא חצי לוג שמן וחוציהו — take the half לוג and divide it in half. חציו לחלות ורקיקין — one half, a רביעית, goes to the חלות and רקיקין together. וחציו לרביכה — and the other רביעית goes to the רביכה alone. A רביעית is roughly three and a half ounces; one might reflect on how small this amount is relative to the flour involved. The thirty מצה loaves are made from עשרה עשרונות — approximately ten five-pound bags of flour — and the entire oil allotment for all of them is a half לוג, amounting to about six ounces total.

The ten loaves of חמץ that are also part of the תודה have their own separate half לוג. And of the thirty מצה loaves, which are ten each of חלות, רקיקין, and רביכה, each group of ten is made from שלשה ושליש עשרונות. The twenty loaves of חלות and רקיקין share one רביעית, while the ten רביכה loaves receive the other רביעית — hence twice as much oil proportionally for the רביכה, though proportionally it remains tiny in comparison to the standard מנחה. As for which loaves precisely receive more based on the sequence of the verse, the גמרא does not resolve this definitively.

### Halacha L'Moshe

Rabbi Elazar ben Azaryah responds to Rabbi Akiva with characteristic directness: עקיבא, אם אתה מרבה כל היום כולו בשמן בשמן איני שומע לך — you can multiply בשמנים all day long, I will not accept it. This is not how we know these things. Rashi understands Rabbi Elazar ben Azaryah to be bothered specifically by the technical point the גמרא raised — that it is not truly ריבוי אחר ריבוי, since the first בשמן functions for its own sake. But the objection seems more fundamental: there is no reliable path from textual nuances of this kind to precise quantitative conclusions, and Rabbi Akiva's method of deriving exact שיעורים from the repetition of words is simply not accepted by all תנאים.

This exchange is part of a recognizable pattern in the גמרא. It is not always the same תנא who challenges Rabbi Akiva — sometimes it is Rabbi Yishmael, sometimes others — but Rabbi Akiva is consistently the one addressed. His approach, according to the גמרא, extended even to דרשת הקוצו של יוד, finding legal significance in the smallest textual details. Other תנאים did not reject his conclusions outright in all cases, but insisted that you cannot reach those conclusions through hermeneutics alone. Perhaps the ruling is known from tradition; perhaps it is learned elsewhere. But you will not get everything from textual derivation alone.

### Mesorah & Shiurim

Rabbi Elazar ben Azaryah then states what these שיעורים truly are: חצי לוג שמן לתודה — the half לוג for the תודה; ורביעית שמן לנזיר — the quarter לוג for the נזיר's מנחה, which is also far less than the standard לוג per עשרון; ואחד עשר יום שבין נדה לנדה — and the eleven days between נדה cycles. All of these are הלכה למשה מסיני. They come from tradition, not from hermeneutical derivation.

The inclusion of the eleven days warrants brief explanation, particularly since it will be treated at length in מסכת נדה. According to the תורה, when a woman bleeds during her menstrual cycle, she is a נדה for seven days, after which she immerses in the מקווה. If she bleeds outside her menstrual period — בלא עת נדתה — and does so for three consecutive days, she becomes a זבה, requiring seven clean days before immersion. The question is: what constitutes "outside her menstrual period"? The answer, transmitted as הלכה למשה מסיני, is that after the seven days of נדה, there is an eleven-day window during which any bleeding is considered non-menstrual. Any bleeding after those eleven days may again be menstrual. Together, this implies an assumed minimum menstrual cycle of eighteen days.

It is worth noting that there is indeed a ספרא that attempts to derive the eleven days from the פסוקים, but that discussion belongs to מסכת נדה.

The broader methodological point here is a rich one. There is a genuine debate, both among ראשונים and scholars generally, about whether the חכמים began with knowledge of the הלכה and worked backward to find textual support — אסמכתא — or whether they were actually deriving the law from the text. The truth is probably somewhere in between and varies by case. When a real מחלוקת turns on how to read a פסוק, one gets the sense of genuine derivation. In other cases, the sense is stronger that the conclusion was known and the verse is being recruited in its support.

The Rambam addressed this indirectly in his discussion of גזירה שוה. He was troubled by the fact that the גמרא requires a מסורה for a גזירה שוה to be used, yet the גמרא still entertains questions like "why derive it this way rather than that way?" — which would make no sense if the specific derivation were itself the מסורה. The Rambam's resolution is that the מסורה is not for the particular גזירה שוה, but for the principle that the law may be derived through גזירה שוה — while the specific construction of the derivation remains open to analysis. This represents a middle position between pure אסמכתא and genuine textual derivation: the conclusion is known, the general method of derivation is authorized, and the work of the תנאים is to identify the most coherent path between the text and the known result.

## Log Per Mincha

### Fixed Log Measure

The משנה states that one always measures with a לוג, even for a מנחה of sixty עשרונות — one does not use a larger measure but simply measures with a לוג sixty times. This parallels the rule for solid measures, where the only vessel is an עשרון: even for a large מנחה, one measures out sixty times.

### Metzora Paradigm

The ברייתא establishes: במצורע עני כתיב עשרון סולת בלול בשמן ולוג שמן — it says of the poor מצורע, one עשרון of flour mixed with oil, and a לוג of oil. לימד על עשרון שטעון לוג — this teaches that each עשרון requires a לוג. That is the position of the חכמים.

The verse in question reads: ואם דל הוא ואין ידו משגת ולקח כבש אחד אשם לתנופה לכפר עליו ועשרון סולת אחד בלול בשמן למנחה ולג שמן — if he is poor, he brings one lamb for an אשם, one עשרון of flour mixed with oil for a מנחה, and a לוג of oil. It must be noted that this לוג of oil is not what is mixed into the מנחה; it is used for the ritual of the מצורע itself, applied to the ear and so on. Yet because the verse juxtaposes an עשרון with a לוג, we use it as the paradigm establishing a לוג per עשרון for all מנחות. This is not the plain meaning of the verse — it is a דרשה — but that is how the ratio is derived.

### Log Ratio Debate

Rabbi Nechemia and Rabbi Eliezer disagree: אפילו מנחה של ששים עשרונות אין לה אלא לוגה — even a מנחה of sixty עשרונות has only one לוג. This follows from reading the verse as למנחה ולוג שמן — not "one עשרון and a לוג" but "the מנחה and a לוג," meaning no matter the size of the מנחה, it receives one לוג.

The גמרא then asks: what do Rabbi Nechemia and Rabbi Eliezer do with the juxtaposition of עשרון and לוג? They respond that ההוא לגופיה — that mention of עשרון is needed to state the law of the מצורע itself: the תורה is telling us that the מצורע brings a מנחה of one עשרון. It is not teaching the לוג-per-עשרון ratio.

The חכמים counter: לגופיה לא צריך — the תורה did not need to specify the עשרון here. One could have derived it by analogy: the non-poor מצורע brings three animals and a מנחה of three עשרונות; the poor מצורע brings one animal of consequence — the אשם — and the others are replaced by birds. In a parallel fashion, the מנחה would reduce proportionally to one עשרון. Thus the תורה need not have stated עשרון, and the fact that it does so links עשרון to לוג as a general teaching.

Rabbi Nechemia and Rabbi Eliezer respond: איצטריך — the תורה did need to state the עשרון, because one might have thought that since הואיל וחס רחמנא עליה לאתויי בדלות — the תורה showed compassion on this poor מצורע by allowing him to bring less — אימא לא ליבעי מיניה כלל — perhaps the מנחה requirement is waived entirely. The חכמים rejoin: לגמרי לא אשכחן — we never find the תורה eliminating a requirement entirely; it only substitutes. Since the תורה replaced two sheep with two birds rather than eliminating them, it would never simply drop the מנחה.

This entire dispute thus turns on whether the word עשרון is redundant. If it is redundant, it serves to establish the universal ratio of לוג per עשרון. If it is not redundant — if it was needed to specify that the מצורע brings one עשרון at all — then it establishes only the local law of the מצורע and teaches nothing about the universal ratio.

### Derivation Methods

This discussion opens onto a broader methodological question that is worth dwelling upon. As the text of the תורה does not provide a שיעור for how much oil goes into a מנחה — it simply says the flour is mixed with oil — the entire enterprise of establishing a שיעור requires a derivation, and that derivation comes from this one case of the מצורע, where the לוג is technically not the amount mixed into the מנחה at all, but is the separate לוג used for the ritual. The juxtaposition alone is the basis for establishing the ratio. Then there is a further dispute as to what that ratio even is.

One might reasonably conclude that in cases such as this, the חכמים knew the הלכה and were working to identify its textual grounding. This is the middle position the Rambam implies: not pure אסמכתא, in which the derivation is entirely post-hoc and decorative, and not pure textual derivation, in which the law is unknown until the verse is analyzed. Rather, there is a tradition that the law is derivable from the text, and the work of derivation — though not free-form — proceeds with a known destination in mind.

The גמרא's final statement here is worth noting: when the משנה says it teaches about the minimum size of a מנחה — that it cannot be less than an עשרון — this follows from למנחה ולוג: something that requires a לוג must by definition be at least an עשרון. Rabbi Nechemia and Rabbi Eliezer extract two teachings from this phrase simultaneously: the minimum size of a מנחה, and the rule that a מנחה of any size receives only one לוג.

## Nesachim Ratios

### Hin Conversion

The משנה taught: ששה לפר ארבעה לאיל שלשה לכבש — six לוגין for an ox, four for a ram, three for a lamb. These are derived from the rule that a הין is twelve לוגין: half a הין for a פר is six, a third for an איל is four, a quarter for a כבש is three. The question is: how do we know a הין is twelve לוגין?

The verse states שמן זית הין — the oil used for the שמן המשחה was a הין. A second verse states שמן משחת קודש יהיה זה לי לדורותיכם — this shall be the holy oil of anointing. The word זה בגימטריא תריסר הוי — the numerical value of זה is twelve (zayin = 7, heh = 5), hence a הין is twelve לוגין. One might ask why a scriptural verse is needed for what might seem to be a self-evident conversion between known measures, but the תורה mentions both לוג and הין without ever stating the conversion factor explicitly. Rather than rely on transmitted knowledge alone, the גמרא anchors the conversion in a textual derivation — though here too, one presumes this is effectively an אסמכתא.

### Menorah Night Oil

The משנה also taught: שלשה ומחצה למנורה חצי לוג לכל נר — three and a half לוגין for the מנורה, a half לוג per lamp. The ברייתא derives this from the verse מערב עד בוקר — the oil must be sufficient to burn from evening through the morning. The teaching is twofold: first, that the oil must be measured to last the full night; and second, that the lighting of the מנורה is אין לך עבודה שכשירה מערב עד בוקר אלא זו בלבד — the only עבודה that is valid specifically from evening through the night. ושיערו חכמים חצי לוג מאורתא ועד צפרא — the חכמים estimated that a half לוג per lamp, half a לוג from evening until morning, is the required amount.

### Estimation Methods

The גמרא records two views on how this estimation was actually conducted. איכא דאמרי מלמעלה למטה שיערו — some say they began with a larger amount and reduced it incrementally, and איכא דאמרי מלמטה למעלה שיערו — others say they began with a smaller amount and increased it.

The rationale behind each approach reflects competing values. According to the view that they began with less and worked upward: התורה חסה על ממונן של ישראל — since the oil, once sanctified in a כלי שרת, could not be discarded if extra remained, the concern was not to waste consecrated oil, and therefore one would approach the minimum cautiously from below. According to the view that they began with more and worked downward: אין עניות במקום עשירות — one should not behave with a spirit of poverty in a place that is to express blessing and abundance. Rather than minimizing carefully, one expresses generosity and works down from abundance.

It is worth noting, as the ירושלמי records, that in the summer months when the nights are short, thicker wicks were used so the oil would not continue burning into the next day, while in the winter thinner wicks were used to ensure the oil lasted through the longer nights. The חכמים's estimation of the half לוג was thus calibrated for an average night, with the wick size adjusted seasonally.

### Tosafot Challenge

תוספות raises a pointed question on the phrase ושיערו חכמים: למה היה צריך לקדש השמן בכלי שרת? Why was it necessary to sanctify the מנורה oil in a כלי שרת at all? The גמרא had already assumed that the measuring vessels used for the מנורה oil served as כלי שרת, and that this was in fact the only reason a half-לוג measure was needed as a כלי שרת.

Tosafot's question cuts to something fundamental. Normally a כלי שרת is required because the object being sanctified is a קרבן — flour is offered, wine is poured, oil is mixed into a מנחה. But the oil of the מנורה is not being offered. One is not quantitatively sacrificing a certain amount; the goal is to achieve light. If it is a short night, less oil suffices. This is unlike a מנחה or a wine libation, where precise quantities are intrinsic to the act of offering. The proof is precisely the שיעור discussion: had this been an offering of a fixed quantity, the question of the night's length would be irrelevant.

This observation sharpens a broader distinction: the הדלקת המנורה is an עבודה performed in the מקדש, but it may not be a קרבן in the technical sense. This distinction has implications elsewhere — for example, in the discussion of why the principle of טומאה הותרה בציבור was not invoked to override the need for pure oil at the time of the miracle of Chanukah. If the מנורה is not a קרבן, the rules governing public offerings in a state of impurity may simply not apply. Tosafot's brief remark thus opens onto a significant question about the nature of the מנורה service.

## Mixing Minchot

### Par-Ayil Mix OK

The משנה taught: מערבין נסכי פר עם נסכי אילים — one may mix the מנחות of oxen with those of rams. This is because the ratio of flour to oil is consistent between them: three עשרונות for a פר with six לוגין of oil and wine, two עשרונות for an איל with four לוגין — both maintain a two-to-one ratio. If those מנחות get mixed together, the proportion of oil to flour has not changed, and one may offer them together on the מזבח.

### Keves Mix Forbidden

By contrast, the כבש receives three עשרונות of flour with three לוגין of oil and wine — a one-to-one ratio, rather than two-to-one. Mixing the מנחה of a כבש with that of a פר or איל disrupts the proportions: one becomes too oil-rich and the other too dry. Hence אבל אין מערבין נסכי כבשים בנסכי פרים ואילים — one may not mix the מנחה of lambs with that of oxen or rams.

The משנה also specifies that this applies regardless of whether the קרבן is an individual's or communal, and even when mixing offerings from different days — since the מנחה accompanying a קרבן does not need to be brought on the same day as the קרבן itself: מנחתם ונסכיהם אפילו מכאן ועד עשרה ימים, it may be brought up to ten days later. Provided the מנחות being mixed are of the same proportional type, they may be combined.

### Blila First

The משנה adds a crucial qualification: ואם בללם אלו בפני עצמן ואלו בפני עצמן ונתערבו כשרים — if one first mixed each type of dough separately — the flour with its appropriate oil — and they subsequently became intermingled, the result is כשר. But אם עד שלא בלל פסול — if the mixing occurred before the individual blendings, it is פסול.

The governing principle is that the מצוה of בלילה — the proper mixing of flour with oil in the correct proportions — must be fulfilled. Once each portion of dough has been mixed correctly, it is valid; the subsequent intermingling does not invalidate it even if proportions become confused, because the obligation has already been discharged.

### Burn Separately

The גמרא raises a challenge. A ברייתא derives from the verse והקטירו — written in the singular — that שלא יערב חלבים בחלבים: one must not mix the fats of one sacrifice with those of another. More broadly, this teaches that anything burned on the מזבח must be burned separately. How, then, does our משנה permit mixing מנחות — which are ultimately burned — even when they are of the same proportional type?

Rabbi Yochanan suggests reading the permissive language of the משנה as אי נתערבו — referring to what happens if they become mixed, not to active mixing לכתחילה. However, the גמרא objects: if so, the prohibitive language later in the משנה — אין מערבין נסכי כבשים בנסכי פרים — would mean that if they do become mixed, they are invalid בדיעבד. But the end of the משנה shows otherwise: ונתערבו כשרים — they are כשר, precisely in the בדיעבד case of having been accidentally mixed. מכלל דרישא לכתחילה קאמר — the opening clause therefore must be stating a לכתחילה permission to mix. The contradiction with the principle of burning separately thus stands.

### Wine Mixing Criteria

Abaye resolves the difficulty with a reinterpretation of the משנה. The prohibition of והקטירו — burning separately — applies to הקטרה, the actual burning. Wine is poured out but not burned; therefore, wine from different sacrifices may be mixed without violating this principle. Oil and flour, however, are burned as part of the מנחה and must be kept separate.

In practice, one would want to maintain this distinction consistently: do not mix wines lest you come to mix flour and oil as well. But in two circumstances, this concern falls away. First: היכא דהוקטר סולת ושמן — once the flour and oil have already been burned, there is no longer any risk of coming to mix them, and the wine may therefore be mixed לכתחילה. Second: if the flour and oil happened to get mixed — and were of matching proportions and hence still valid — then mixing their wines is permitted as well.

Abaye thus reads the משנה as referring throughout to wine, not to the flour-and-oil מנחה itself. The active mixing permitted by the משנה is the mixing of wines, either after the מנחות have been burned or under the condition that the מנחות themselves have already gotten mixed and are כשר. Mixing the flour and oil מנחות actively and לכתחילה remains prohibited on account of the rule of הקטרה.

The intermingling of the second half of the משנה — where pre-mixed doughs that subsequently got mixed together are כשר — remains as a separate teaching about the timing requirement of בלילה: the key moment is when the dough is properly constituted, not when it is burned.

## Omer Lamb Offering

### Double Flour Only

The final section of the משנה concerns the lamb brought with the עומר. The תורה specifies שני עשרונים for this lamb's מנחה — double the normal amount of flour. The question is whether the oil and wine are similarly doubled. The ברייתא states: יכול כשם שמנחתו כפולה כך יינו כפול — perhaps since the flour is doubled, the wine is also doubled? The verse answers: ונסכו יין רביעית ההין — the wine is explicitly a quarter הין, which is three לוגין, the standard amount for a lamb. The wine is not doubled.

One might then propose: יכול לא יהיה יינו כפול שאין נבלל עם מנחתו — wine is not doubled because it is not blended with the flour; but שמנו כפול שנבלל עם מנחתו — the oil should be doubled since it is mixed with the flour, and the general principle of the חכמים is that more flour requires more oil. The verse answers again: ונסכו — the full לוג, all of its liquid measures, לא יהיה אלא רביעית — none of them are doubled; all remain at the standard רביעית ההין.

### Niskah-Nisko Equation

The גמרא asks where this is derived. Rabbi Elazar states: כתיב ונסכה וקרינן ונסכו — the word is written with a ה but read with a ו. How are both readings meaningful? נסכה דמנחה כנסכו דיין — the נסכה of the מנחה, meaning its liquid component, is equated with the נסכו of the wine. Just as the wine libation is a רביעית ההין, so too the oil of the מנחה: מה יין רביעית אף שמן נמי רביעית — the oil, like the wine, remains at the standard quantity and is not doubled along with the flour.

## Sheelo Lishmo Status

### Asham Identity

Rabbi Yochanan states: אשם מצורע ששחטו שלא לשמו טעון נסכים — an אשם מצורע that was slaughtered שלא לשמו still requires its accompanying מנחה. The reasoning: שאם אי אתה אומר כן פסלתו — if you do not say this, you will have invalidated it. Since an אשם מצורע that is brought without its מנחה is invalid, and this sacrifice is still valid as a קרבן even though it does not discharge the owner's obligation, it must retain its defining identity as an אשם מצורע and therefore must be accompanied by its מנחה.

The underlying question is significant. Normally an אשם does not come with נסכים; the מנחה here is unique to the מצורע ritual, as we read earlier in the daf. When such a קרבן is brought שלא לשמו, it is valid but does not fulfill the owner's obligation. Does it then retain its original identity as an אשם מצורע, with all the attendant requirements? Or does it shed that identity and become a generic אשם — which would require no מנחה?

### Identity Retention Test

Rav Menashya bar Gadata challenges Rabbi Yochanan with a series of parallel cases. If the principle is that a קרבן brought שלא לשמו retains its full original identity and therefore all its requirements must be maintained — otherwise it would be invalidated — then consider: כבש הבא עם העומר ששחטו שלא לשמו תהא מנחתו כפולה — the lamb of the עומר brought שלא לשמו should require the doubled מנחה of שני עשרונות, for otherwise you would invalidate it. Similarly: תמיד של שחר ששחטו שלא לשמו יהא טעון שני גזירים ושני כהנים — the morning תמיד brought שלא לשמו should require the two logs of wood and two כהנים associated with the תמיד offering, since the גמרא assumes these logs of wood are an essential part of the תמיד. Likewise for the afternoon תמיד. Each case would equally have to retain its full original character, with all attendant requirements.

The force of the challenge is that it extends the principle to conclusions that seem difficult to sustain. If the identity is fully retained in every case, the consequences ramify broadly.

### Ein Hachi Nami

The initial response recorded in the גמרא is simply: אין הכי נמי — indeed, that is precisely the case. What appears to be a reductio ad absurdum is answered with a matter-of-fact affirmation: yes, even when a קרבן is brought שלא לשמו, it retains its full original identity, and all its requirements — the double מנחה for the עומר lamb, the logs of wood for the תמיד — must be met. If they are not, the קרבן is invalidated.

Tomorrow, Abaye will challenge this position and argue that a קרבן brought שלא לשמו does in fact shed its original identity, becoming generic in character. The tension between these two views — full retention of identity versus a shift to generic status — is the substantive question with which we close.
